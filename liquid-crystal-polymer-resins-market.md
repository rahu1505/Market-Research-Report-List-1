<p><p>Liquid crystal polymer resins are classified based on their High Deflection Temperature (HDT) which determines their thermal stability. The market types include HDT Above 300℃, suitable for demanding high-temperature applications; HDT Between 240℃ and 300℃, commonly used in automotive and electrical industries; HDT Between 200℃ and 240℃, ideal for electronic connectors and housings; and HDT Below 200℃, primarily used for general-purpose applications. These classifications ensure that different industries can choose the appropriate liquid crystal polymer resin based on their required temperature resistance and performance needs.</p></p>
