<p><p>Electron microscopy, commonly used in various industrial applications, encompasses different market types such as Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM), Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM), and others. TEM utilizes electron beams to pass through specimens, providing high-resolution images and detailed information regarding material structure, composition, and defects. SEM, on the other hand, scans the specimen's surface with electron beams to create topographic images, enabling analysis of surface morphology, particle size, and elemental composition. Other electron microscopy techniques include STEM (Scanning Transmission Electron Microscopy) and EELS (Electron Energy Loss Spectroscopy), contributing to a wide range of industrial research and analysis.</p></p>
