<p><p>Freeze-dried food packaging can be categorized into two main types: rigid packaging and flexible packaging. Rigid packaging refers to containers made of hard materials like glass or plastic that are sturdy and strong. It provides excellent protection for freeze-dried food, ensuring its freshness and preventing damage. On the other hand, flexible packaging consists of materials like plastic films or laminates that are pliable and can be easily manipulated. It offers convenience and portability for consumers, making it suitable for on-the-go consumption. Both types play a crucial role in preserving and delivering freeze-dried food products to the market.</p></p>
