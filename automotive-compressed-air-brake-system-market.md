<p><p>The automotive compressed air brake system market consists of two main types: drum brake and disc brake. Drum brakes are a type of braking system that use a rotating drum and brake shoes to slow down or stop a vehicle. They are cost-effective and commonly used in commercial vehicles. On the other hand, disc brakes use brake pads and a rotor to generate friction and stop the vehicle. They offer better performance, precision, and heat dissipation, making them popular in passenger cars and high-performance vehicles.</p></p>
