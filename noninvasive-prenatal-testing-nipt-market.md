<p><p>Noninvasive Prenatal Testing (NIPT) is a screening method used to detect genetic abnormalities in a fetus without posing any risk to the mother or baby. Two types of NIPT are DNA testing and blood testing. DNA testing examines fetal DNA present in the mother's blood to identify chromosomal abnormalities, such as Down syndrome. Blood testing analyzes the mother's blood for specific markers, proteins, or hormones that indicate the presence of fetal abnormalities. Both methods are safe, accurate, and help in making informed decisions regarding the pregnancy.</p></p>
