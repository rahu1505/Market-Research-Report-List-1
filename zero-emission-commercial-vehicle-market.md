<p><p>Battery Electric Commercial Vehicles (BECVs) are a type of zero-emission commercial vehicle that rely solely on electric energy stored in their batteries for propulsion. They produce zero tailpipe emissions and are typically charged by plugging them into an electric power source.</p><p>Fuel Cell Commercial Vehicles (FCCVs) are another type of zero-emission commercial vehicle that use hydrogen to generate electricity through a chemical reaction with oxygen. This electricity then powers the vehicle's electric motor, producing only water vapor as a byproduct. FCCVs require refueling with hydrogen gas, which can be obtained from various sources such as natural gas or renewable energy.</p></p>
