<p><p>Microfibrillated Cellulose (MFC) is a versatile material produced from cellulose fibers with a high aspect ratio, providing exceptional strength and flexibility. Different market types exist for its production. In the mechanical method, cellulose is physically treated through high-pressure homogenization, resulting in MFC particles. The chemical method involves the use of chemicals to dissolve the cellulose and later regenerate it into MFC. Lastly, the biological method employs enzymes or bacteria to break down cellulose into MFC. These various approaches cater to different industrial needs and applications of MFC.</p></p>
