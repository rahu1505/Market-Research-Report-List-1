<p><strong>The Connected Car Devices Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Vehicle-to-Vehicle (V2V)</li><li>Vehicle-to-Infrastructure (V2I)</li><li>Vehicle-to-Pedestrian(V2P)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Connected car devices refer to technological tools that enable communication and data sharing between vehicles and various entities. The market is categorized into three types: Vehicle-to-Vehicle (V2V) involves cars exchanging information to enhance safety and efficiency on the road. Vehicle-to-Infrastructure (V2I) enables vehicles to share data with infrastructure systems like traffic signals and road signs to optimize traffic flow. Vehicle-to-Pedestrian (V2P) facilitates communication between vehicles and pedestrians, enhancing safety measures such as warning alerts. These market types aim to improve overall transportation effectiveness, safety, and convenience.</p></p>

<p><p>In the connected car devices market, there are two main applications: Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) and Aftermarket. OEM refers to devices that are built into vehicles by the manufacturer during production. These devices come pre-installed with features like navigation systems, infotainment systems, and advanced safety features. Aftermarket devices, on the other hand, are installed in vehicles after they have been manufactured. These devices include things like smartphone connectivity adapters, GPS trackers, and smart Dashcams. Both OEM and Aftermarket devices cater to the growing demand for smart and connected features in vehicles.</p></p>
