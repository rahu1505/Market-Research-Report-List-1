<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The automotive elastomers market is divided into two main types: synthetic automotive elastomers and thermoplastic automotive elastomers. Synthetic automotive elastomers are manufactured through chemical processes and possess high elasticity, durability, and resistance to heat, chemicals, and abrasion. They are commonly used in applications such as automotive hoses, gaskets, and seals. On the other hand, thermoplastic automotive elastomers are made from petroleum-based or synthetic materials and offer excellent flexibility, high impact resistance, and easy processing capabilities. These elastomers are commonly found in automotive interiors, exterior trims, and under-the-hood applications.</p></p>
