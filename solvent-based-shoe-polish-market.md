<p><p>Solvent-based shoe polish market types can be classified into two categories: Type I and Type II. Type I refers to shoe polishes that are made up of solvents like turpentine or naphtha, which evaporate quickly, leaving a glossy finish on the shoes. These polishes are typically used for formal leather shoes and offer a high shine. On the other hand, Type II polishes contain solvents like petroleum distillates, which take longer to evaporate, leaving a thicker and more durable protective coating. Type II polishes are often used for work boots or shoes that endure rough conditions.</p></p>
