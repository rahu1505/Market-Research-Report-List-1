<p><p>The stationary Li-ion battery market can be categorized into three types: Li-Ni, Li-Ni-Co, and Li-Mn. Li-Ni batteries consist of lithium and nickel elements, providing a high energy density and longer lifespan. Li-Ni-Co batteries include cobalt, which enhances their capacity and stability. These batteries are commonly used in electric vehicles and energy storage systems. Li-Mn batteries, combining manganese with lithium, offer good thermal stability and safety. They are often found in medical devices, power tools, and renewable energy applications. These variations cater to different needs for energy storage in stationary applications.</p></p>
