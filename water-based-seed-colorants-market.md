<p><p>Water-based seed colorants are used to enhance the appearance of seeds and improve their marketability. The market for these colorants can be categorized into two types: dyes and pigments. Dyes are water-soluble colorants that penetrate into the seed coat, resulting in a translucent or transparent color. On the other hand, pigments are insoluble particles that adhere to the seed surface, creating an opaque and vibrant color. Both dyes and pigments offer a wide range of colors to choose from, allowing seed producers to customize their products and attract consumers.</p></p>
