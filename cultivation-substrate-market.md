<p><p>Cultivation substrate refers to the material used for growing plants. The market for cultivation substrates can be categorized into different types. Soilless mixes involve using a mixture of organic materials, like peat moss or coconut coir, ensuring good drainage and aeration. Composts are made from decomposed organic matter, providing nutrients to the plants. Gravel substrates assist in drainage and root stability. Inert media, typically made of materials like perlite or vermiculite, do not provide nutrients but ensure good aeration. Other types of substrates may include materials like rockwool, sand, or clay pellets, depending on specific cultivation needs.</p></p>
