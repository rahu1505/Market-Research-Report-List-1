<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The high-pressure polyethylene market can be broadly categorized into two types: the autoclave method and the tube method. In the autoclave method, the polymerization reaction occurs in a high-pressure reactor, resulting in the production of high-density polyethylene. On the other hand, the tube method involves the use of a tubular reactor, where the polymerization reaction takes place at high temperatures and pressures to yield high-pressure polyethylene. These two methods differ in their reactor designs and production processes, ultimately leading to variations in the properties and applications of the polyethylene produced.</p></p>
