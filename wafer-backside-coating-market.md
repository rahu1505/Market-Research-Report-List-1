<p><p>The wafer backside coating market is divided into two main types: conductive and non-conductive. Conductive coatings are formulated with materials that have properties allowing the flow of electricity, making them suitable for electronic applications. These coatings are commonly used in semiconductor manufacturing to provide electrical grounding and facilitate heat dissipation. On the other hand, non-conductive coatings are designed to insulate the wafer's backside and protect it from various environmental factors, such as moisture and contaminants. They are widely used in industries where electrical conductivity is not required, such as pharmaceutical and optoelectronic applications.</p></p>
