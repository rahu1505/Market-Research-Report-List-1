<p><p>Heat transfer fluids are substances used in semiconductor processes to efficiently transfer heat from one point to another. Perfluoroalkoxy (PFAS) and perfluoropolyether (PFPE) are two types of heat transfer fluids commonly used in this market. PFAS fluids are known for their high thermal stability and resistance to aggressive chemicals, making them ideal for demanding semiconductor processes. On the other hand, PFPE fluids offer excellent lubrication properties and low vapor pressure, enabling them to operate under high temperatures and vacuum conditions. Both types of fluids play a crucial role in maintaining optimal operating temperatures and ensuring the longevity of semiconductor equipment.</p></p>
