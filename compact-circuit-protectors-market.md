Type I refers to circuit breakers that provide protection against overcurrents by opening the circuit when a fault occurs. These can handle high inrush currents and are commonly used in residential and commercial applications. Type II circuit protectors, on the other hand, combine the functions of Type I and Type II surge protectors, safeguarding against not only overcurrents but also voltage surges caused by lightning or other electrical disturbances. These are typically used in sensitive electronic equipment or appliances.</p></p>
