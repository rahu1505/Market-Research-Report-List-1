<p><p>Automotive Pedestrian Protection Systems (PPS) refer to the technologies and systems incorporated in vehicles to minimize the impact and severity of collisions between vehicles and pedestrians. These systems are designed to detect the presence of pedestrians and reduce the risk of injury through advanced sensing technologies, automatic braking, external airbags, and active hood systems.</p><p>The global Automotive Pedestrian Protection Systems (PPS) market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 6.2% during the forecast period. Factors driving this growth include increasing concerns about pedestrian safety, stringent government regulations for vehicle safety, and the rising adoption of advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS).</p><p>One of the major trends in the market is the integration of pedestrian detection systems with ADAS. Pedestrian detection technology, combined with other ADAS technologies such as forward collision warning (FCW) and automatic emergency braking (AEB), provides better safety by automatically applying brakes or providing alerts to the driver in case of an imminent collision.</p><p>Another trend in the Automotive PPS market is the development of active hood systems. These systems use sensors to detect if a pedestrian has been struck by the vehicle, and the hood is lifted in milliseconds to create a cushioning effect and reduce the impact on the pedestrian's body.</p><p>Geographically, the market is witnessing significant growth in regions such as North America and Europe, owing to the presence of stringent safety regulations and the high adoption rate of advanced safety technologies.
