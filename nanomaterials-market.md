<p><p>Nanomaterials are classified into various types based on their composition and properties. Carbon-based nanomaterials include graphene and carbon nanotubes, which possess exceptional strength and conductivity. Metals, such as silver and gold nanoparticles, exhibit unique optical and catalytic properties. Dendrimers are highly branched synthetic molecules with precise structures, suitable for drug delivery systems. Nanoclay consists of layered mineral silicates, used in coatings and composites. Nanocellulose, derived from cellulose fibers, has high strength and is used in applications like paper and biofuels. These diverse types of nanomaterials offer immense potential for various industries and scientific research.</p></p>
