<p><p>Real-time locating systems (RTLS) are used to track and locate objects or people in real-time. There are four main types of RTLS technology: RFID, Wi-Fi, UWB, and GPS. </p><p>RFID (Radio Frequency Identification) uses radio signals to identify and track objects with tags that have a unique identification number. </p><p>Wi-Fi (Wireless Fidelity) RTLS uses Wi-Fi signals to determine the location of objects or people by detecting the signal strength and analyzing it.</p><p>UWB (Ultra-Wideband) RTLS uses short-range radio waves to accurately track location, allowing for high precision and accuracy.</p><p>GPS (Global Positioning System) RTLS relies on satellite signals to determine the location of objects or people on the Earth's surface.</p></p>
