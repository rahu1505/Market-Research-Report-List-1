<p><p>Tractor attachments and implements generally fall into two market types: powered and unpowered. Powered attachments are designed to be operated by the tractor's power source, such as the engine or power take-off (PTO). Examples include mowers, hay balers, and irrigation pumps. Unpowered attachments, on the other hand, rely on manual operation or external power sources for their functionality. Examples of unpowered attachments are plows, harrows, and seed drills. Both powered and unpowered attachments play crucial roles in enhancing the tractor's versatility and capability for various agricultural tasks.</p></p>
