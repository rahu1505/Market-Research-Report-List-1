<p><p>The EV dielectric fluid market can be categorized into two types based on their viscosity: low viscosity and high viscosity fluids. Low viscosity fluids have a thinner consistency, allowing them to flow more easily. These fluids are preferred in applications where fast heat dissipation is required. On the other hand, high viscosity fluids are thicker and provide better heat resistance. They are commonly used in high-temperature applications where efficient insulation and thermal stability are crucial. Both low and high viscosity fluids play essential roles in various electrical vehicle systems, providing necessary electrical insulation and thermal management.</p></p>
